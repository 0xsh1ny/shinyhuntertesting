How recognition works & accuracy notes
This runs entirely in your browser. Hand tracking uses Google's pretrained MediaPipe Hands model (21 landmarks per hand). Letters are then identified by a geometric rule-based classifier — not a trained letter-classification network — so accuracy varies by letter shape:

reliable B · C · D · F · G · H · I · K · L · O · U · V · W · Y
trickier A · E · M · N · S · T (closed-fist shapes — differ only by subtle thumb position)

J and Z are not included — they require motion, which this frame-by-frame classifier can't capture. Hold a shape steady for about a second to lock in a letter. Good, even lighting and keeping your hand 30–60cm from the camera helps a lot.
